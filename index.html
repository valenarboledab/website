Process

A vision brought
to life, step by step.

A comprehensive process that integrates design, precision and construction to create a framework for exceptional projects.

01
Concept & Masterplan
We begin with a deep understanding of the site, its context and potential. A masterplan is developed to organize the program around nature, connectivity and experience.
02
Schematic Design
The architecture takes shape. Volumes, circulation, spatial strategies and landscape integration are defined to create a harmonious relationship between buildings and nature.
03
Design Development
Materials, structure, systems and details are refined. Every decision ensures functionality, sustainability and timeless quality aligned with the design intent.
04
Construction Documentation
Detailed plans, specifications and coordination documents produced to guide construction with clarity and accuracy at every phase.
05
Construction & Execution
On site, the vision becomes reality. We work closely with contractors and consultants to ensure quality, timing and faithful execution of every detail.
06
Completion & Impact
A project that transforms the site and enhances the lives of those who experience it — a legacy of architecture, nature and community.
07

Interior Design Process

From feeling
to finished space.

My interior design process is built around one belief: a space should first make you feel something, then function beautifully around that feeling.

Case Study · Spain

Edificio Burgos — Reforma Integral · 5 pisos · Burgos, Castilla y León

Complete interior design of a 5-story residential building — 3 units, 2 penthouses and common areas.

01

First Step

Brief & Project Scope

Every project starts with a deep conversation — not about dimensions, but about how the client wants to feel in their space. Lifestyle, references, what bothers them about the current space, and what they dream for the new one.

From that I define the Project Scope: spaces to intervene, style direction, budget range, and timeline.

Edificio Burgos scope:

— 3 viviendas + 2 buhardillas + zona común — Style: Japandi · Orgánico · Mediterráneo — Sala, comedor, cocina, cuartos, baños per floor — Aesthetic coherence across all 5 floors
02

Second Step

Moodboard by Space

I build a separate moodboard for each space: bedrooms, living rooms, kitchens and bathrooms. Each captures emotion, textures, shapes, lighting direction and color palette specific to that space.

03

Third Step

Space Planning & Colorimetry

With the moodboard approved, I move into spatial planning — laying out furniture, circulation flows, and functional zones within the actual floor plan in AutoCAD or Revit.

Colorimetry happens simultaneously: exact paint colors, material finishes and surface tones defined for each room based on orientation, size, and mood.

Per room I define:

— Furniture layout— Traffic flow — Paint color (HEX)— Floor finish — Wall treatment— Light zoning
04

Fourth Step

Shopping List

A curated, numbered catalog of every item the space needs — furniture, lighting, fixtures, textiles and objects — each selected to match the moodboard aesthetically, fit the plan dimensionally, and respect the budget.

Final Thoughts · Development

Arboretum:
Designing More
Than a House

Some residential projects begin with square footage, budgets or market comparables. Arboretum began with a different question: How should a place feel?

When I started studying the Lake Oconee market in Georgia, I realized buyers were not simply searching for larger homes or luxury finishes. They were searching for calm. For privacy. For nature. For a slower and more intentional lifestyle that still felt refined and timeless.

That idea became the foundation behind Arboretum. More than a residential proposal, the project became an exploration of how architecture, landscape, visualization and development strategy can work together to shape emotional experience and long-term value simultaneously.

Architecture Aligned With

Market Performance

One of the most important lessons I've learned through development is that good architecture cannot exist disconnected from market reality. Beautiful spaces alone are not enough. The strongest projects balance emotion with strategy.

The proposal for Arboretum was built around two questions: How can we create a space that feels genuinely elevated? And how do we ensure that elevation translates into real, measurable value?

"Spaces that feel elevated while remaining financially intelligent and executable."

Designing for

Emotional Connection

The design process began with a simple goal: to create a home that felt calm, grounded and connected to nature. Every material, proportion and visual decision was selected to support a sense of comfort, privacy and belonging.

The goal was not to create a beautiful object. The goal was to create a place where people felt genuinely at home — in the deepest and most intentional sense of the word.

Visualization as

Strategy

Visualization was not treated as decoration. It became a strategic tool to communicate the lifestyle, atmosphere and value of the project before construction. Through realistic imagery, the project could be understood emotionally and commercially.

"The future of residential development is not about building more. It is about building spaces people genuinely connect with."

Landscape as Part of

The Architecture

At Arboretum, the landscape became part of the architecture itself. Outdoor living, natural textures, framed views and soft transitions between interior and exterior were essential to the project identity.

Development

Thinking

Arboretum was also shaped by development logic. Every decision had to support both beauty and strategy — responding to the market, buyer expectations, construction feasibility and long-term value.

Building with

Intention

Arboretum is about more than building a house. It is about creating a place where architecture, nature and lifestyle work together to create long-term emotional and financial value.

Final Thoughts

Arboretum ultimately became more than
a residential pilot project.

It became a reflection of the type of work I want to continue creating — spaces where architecture, experience and strategy coexist with clarity and purpose.

Because the future of residential development is not about building more. It is about building spaces people genuinely connect with.
